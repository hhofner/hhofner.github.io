My favorite programming fonts

Since there are a lot of fonts out there, I suggest to those who will be spending a lot of time looking at monospaced text to consider changing your font every now and then, to spice things up and make your life just a little bit more fun. Here are some of my favorite fonts that I usually switch between:

Menlo

Let's start with Apples out of the box monospaced font. Most people just stick with this, and it's fine as it especially looks good with the San Francisco font (Apple's default font). Luckily, Nerd Fonts provides their own version with ligatures called MesloLG Nerd Font for those non Apple users.

Jetbrains Mono (Nerd Font)

JetBrains Mono typeface is available under the SIL Open Font License 1.1 license and can be used free of charge, for both commercial and non-commercial purposes. You do not need to give credit to JetBrains, although we will appreciate it very much if you do.
This font is a classic, and often always draws me back in to using it. You can install the Nerd Font version that comes with icons, or just fetch the original from JetBrains themselves.

Input Mono

Link. This is my favorite font. It feels similar to the Berkeley Mono font. I won't add a preview to it as it requires purchase for publishing options, but you can check it out for free for private use.

Cascadia Pro

And finally, I present my last font Cascadia Pro (Nerd Fonts version, of course. It's under the name "CaskaydiaCove Nerd Font".). This font is super fun, and it strikes a wonderful balance with Input Mono, meaning that I particularly like to switch between them.
